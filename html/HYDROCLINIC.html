Nabab Aurangzeb

Nabab Aurangzeb (1618–1707) was the sixth Mughal Emperor, who ruled over most of the Indian subcontinent from 1658 until his death in 1707. He was the third son of Emperor Shah Jahan and is known for his expansionist policies, which led to the largest territorial extent of the Mughal Empire. Aurangzeb was a devout Muslim and implemented strict Islamic laws, which caused tension with the empire's diverse population. His reign is often criticized for its religious intolerance and heavy taxation, which contributed to the decline of the Mughal Empire after his death.
